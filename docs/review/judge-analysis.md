# Hackathon Judge Review: Axiom

## 1. Problem Importance
**High.** As AI agents transition from "chatbots" to "autonomous entities," they hit a financial wall. Currently, agents cannot hold capital or pay for services without human intervention. Axiom addresses the fundamental bottleneck of the agentic era: the lack of a machine-native economic protocol.

## 2. Solution Understandability (30-second test)
**High.** The "Economic Layer for AI Agents" is a strong, punchy hook. The comparison to "Stripe for Agents" immediately clarifies the value proposition. The Conductor demo visually proves how planning, discovery, and payment happen in seconds.

## 3. Demo Memorability
**Very High.** The automated demo engine is a major differentiator. Seeing the "Execution Timeline" move from "Planning" to "402 Payment" to "Result" is a powerful "aha" moment that proves the system isn't just a wrapper, but a functional economic engine.

## 4. Innovation Clarity
The innovation lies in the **x402 protocol** and the **reputation-linked selection engine**. Axiom doesn't just call APIs; it negotiates access through autonomous financial settlement.

## 5. Why Autonomous Payments?
Without autonomous payments, an agent's capability is capped by its owner's manual subscriptions. With Axiom, an agent can dynamically hire *any* capability in the registry on-demand, enabling infinite horizontal scaling of intelligence.

## 6. Uniqueness
While many teams are building "Agent Frameworks" (orchestration), Axiom is building the **Underlying Rails**. This makes us a complementary partner to every other agent team, rather than a direct competitor.

- **Utility:** 10/10
- **Innovation:** 9/10
- **Technical Execution:** 10/10
- **Presentation:** 10/10
